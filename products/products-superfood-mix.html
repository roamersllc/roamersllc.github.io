Our Superfood Mix is a quick and easy way to get some important wholefoods into your diet. A combination of four different seeds, gluten free oats and Indiana peanut butter powder in one convenient package.






In stock, usually dispatched within 24 hours.

A dry mix made with love in small batches locally in Indianapolis. We do not add any fillers, preservatives or additives, and we only use certified organic or non-GMO ingredients.

Each pouch of goodness contains about 28 tablespoons of our favorite smoothie ingredients, which can be added to fresh fruits, veggies and a liquid of your choice to make a wholesome smoothie.

We recommend refrigerating once opened and using within 3 months.

The ingredients in Roamer's Superfood Mix are nutrient dense. But what does that mean? It means that from the calories you are consuming, you are taking in goodness to nourish body for optimal health, versus consuming empty calories with no nutrition which will be stored as excess fat around your organs and body. We all should be eating more nutrient dense wholefoods which heal and protect the body and mind, and less processed, toxic laden, empty calories which cause and accelerate chronic disease and contribute to mental health illnesses.

Essential Fatty Acids

Contribute to eye health, memory, fight depression and anxiety, and promote healthy hormone production. Chia and hemp seeds in particular are an excellent source of plant-based ALA Omega 3. Omega fatty acids in the right ratio (i.e. not overdoing the omega 6 which leads to inflammation) can be anti-inflammatory, lower cholesterol (because they raise HDL, the good cholesterol) and improve metabolic conditions and autoimmune diseases. Omega 3 is essential to brain development during pregnancy and early childhood.

Essential Amino Acids

The mix is about 30% protein; all ingredients are high sources of plant-based protein. Whilst protein is vital to our health (for repair, growth and energy) and wellbeing, far too much emphasis has been put on protein in the media over recent years with the promotion of fad, short term diets, and to the detriment of other food categories such as fiber, carbohydrates, good fats, minerals and vitamins which much of the population lacks in! Virtually none of the developed population will lack protein in their diets (even vegans and vegetarians!) because protein is so readily available and is contained in most foods, and we don’t actually need that much of it to maintain a healthy body. (In fact, a lot of Americans get too much protein which is stored as excess fat. It is thought that a diet which gets in excess of 25% of its calories from protein will be detrimental to long term health for even an active person. Science suggests an inactive person only needs around 15% protein and a moderately active person who exercises several days a week would need around 20%).

Fiber

We need fiber to fill us up and to promote a healthy gut flora and regular bowl movement. Gut health is core to overall health (even mental health!) and impacts our ability to absorb nutrients and to push toxins and excess hormones out of the body. The mix is rich in prebiotics (the food for probiotics, i.e. good bacteria that live in the gut) especially from the oats. Research shows that somewhere in the region of 92%-98% of Americans do not get enough fiber.

Carbohydrates

Do not be afraid! We need carbs! The wholefood kind! Carbs provide our cells with energy by converting it into glucose. Glucose fuels all of our cells to allow the body to function – to breathe, to move, to think. Almost 2/3 of the carbs in the mix are fiber. We should get the majority of our calories from carbs, but choose fiber dense, wholefood carbs like fruits and grains and starchy vegetables, over refined sugar laiden, processed carbs.

Minerals

Important for healthy bones, teeth, nerve transmission, blood flow and blood clotting, muscle function, creating proteins (and enzymes, which speed up chemical reactions in the body), and play important roles in the endocrine system (hormones) and the metabolic system and are needed for normal growth….a varied and balanced diet is your best chance of getting enough minerals. Small amounts of magnesium, iron, zinc, calcium, phosphorus, manganese, magnesium, potassium and selenium are found in the natural ingredients in our mix which will count towards your daily intake.

Vitamins

Vitamins work with minerals to performs hundreds of functions within the body. There are 13 essential vitamins and 8 of these are B-vitamins. Vitamin A, E and some of the B vitamins can be found in our mix. Vitamin A is needed for healthy skin, teeth, bones, mucus membranes and soft tissue. Vitamin E is an antioxidant, helps vitamin K to work and plays a role in the formation of red blood cells. Vitamin B1, B3, B6, folate (B9) all play slightly different roles such as the formation of red bloods cell, maintaining brain function, maintaining healthy nerves, the production of DNA, converting carbohydrates into energy and more.
